### Preparation Phase Description
In the Preparation phase, the client **intends to take action soon** and is actively preparing to make changes. The client is generally motivated but may still experience uncertainty, fear of failure, or practical barriers.

### Therapy Goals for This Phase
The primary goals are:
* Strengthen the client’s commitment to change
* Develop a clear and realistic action plan
* Identify potential barriers and coping strategies
* Increase self-efficacy and confidence
* Prepare the client to implement behavioral changes

**Important constraints:**
* Focus on **planning and preparation**, not long-term treatment yet.
* Plans should be **realistic, collaborative, and gradual**.
* Avoid overwhelming the client with too many changes at once.
