### Contemplation Phase Description
In the Contemplation phase, the client **recognizes that a problem exists** and is beginning to consider change, but feels **ambivalent or conflicted**. The client may simultaneously want to change and fear the consequences of change.

### Therapy Goals for This Phase
The primary goals are:
* Explore and resolve ambivalence about change
* Increase the client’s motivation and readiness
* Help the client examine beliefs and assumptions influencing their behavior
* Strengthen self-efficacy and confidence in the possibility of change
* Begin gentle cognitive restructuring of unhelpful thoughts

**Important constraints:**
* Do NOT push the client directly into action planning yet.
* Focus on **understanding ambivalence and strengthening motivation**.
* Behavioral planning should remain exploratory rather than prescriptive.
