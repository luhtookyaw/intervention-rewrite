### Pre-Contemplation Phase Description
In the Pre-contemplation phase, the client is **not yet ready to change** their behavior. They may feel overwhelmed, defensive, avoidant, or hopeless. They may deny the severity of their problem or believe that change is not possible.

### Therapy Goals for This Phase
The primary goals are:
* Build therapeutic rapport and trust
* Increase awareness of thoughts, emotions, and behavior patterns
* Reduce defensiveness and resistance
* Help the client explore discrepancies between their current situation and personal values
* Encourage reflection without pushing for change

**Important constraints:**
* Do NOT propose action plans or behavior change programs yet.
* Do NOT push the client toward solutions or commitments.
* Focus on **awareness, curiosity, and engagement**.
